Franchise Demo Guide

Internal cheat sheet for the Friday franchise consultant meeting

1. Friday Meeting Talking Points

Open with this question

“When a candidate brings you two FDDs to evaluate, what does your current comparison process look like?”

Let them answer. Most will describe something manual, time-consuming, and inconsistent. That’s the opening.

Key stats to land

14.8 vs 28.5 year payback Item 19 present vs absent 7.41 vs 4.18 overall score −180 units in 3 years (Snap)

Same category. Same buyer profile. Completely different risk. Layer8 makes the difference visible in 60 seconds.

The Item 19 hook

Item 19 is optional. Most consultants know this. What they may not have articulated clearly is: choosing not to provide Item 19 is itself a data point. Snap Fitness made that choice. Layer8 scores it at double weight. That single decision accounts for a significant portion of the score gap between Anytime and Snap.

The question to plant: “How do you currently communicate this risk to a candidate who’s already excited about a brand?”

Show the comparison report first

The FCR (Franchise Comparison Report) is the most immediately compelling piece. It shows the domain-by-domain gap at a glance — no setup required. Start there, then drill into the individual FOE reports for Anytime and Snap to show the depth behind each score.

2. How to Demo

  1. Start at index.html

    Walk through the portfolio overview. Point to the stats bar (2 evaluated, 8 domains, 40 criteria). Show the Item 19 column — green pill vs red pill. Let that land before clicking anything.

  2. Open Anytime Fitness FOE

    Click “View Report” for Anytime Fitness. Walk through the 8 domains. Point to the green Item 19 checkmark, the $395K average unit revenue, the 7.41 overall score. This is what a clean FDD looks like through the Layer8 lens.

  3. Open Snap Fitness FOE

    Click “View Report” for Snap Fitness. Immediately point to the red Item 19 warning. Then walk down to the domain table: unit economics is 2.20/10. Franchisee satisfaction is 3.5. System is contracting. The score tells a coherent story — let the report tell it.

  4. Open the FCR Comparison Report

    Show the domain-by-domain table. Anytime Fitness wins every domain. The winner banner at the top is instant. Then show the Item 19 section with the explicit callout about Snap not providing it — this is the defensible, documented basis for the recommendation.

  5. Close with franchise_explainer.html

    Walk through the three-product vision: FOE, FCR, and FRA (coming). Frame FRA as the complement — once you know the franchise scores high, you assess whether the candidate is right for it. That’s the full consultant workflow.

Tip: Keep the browser at 100% zoom. The reports are designed to be readable at full screen. If you need to present on a projector, increase to 125%.

3. Founding Partner Ask

The Ask

Same framing as the M&A broker ask for ER and DD products. We’re looking for a small number of founding partner franchise consultants who want to be part of building this product category.

  • First evaluation on us — bring us a real candidate and a real FDD
  • 30-minute debrief included — we walk through the report together
  • Founding partner pricing locked in for the first 12 months
  • Early access to FRA (Franchisee Readiness Assessment) when it ships
  • Input into product development — your workflow shapes what we build

4. Pricing Questions

⚠ Do not discuss pricing today.

If asked directly, say: “We’re finalizing the founding partner pricing structure now. What I can tell you is it’s designed to fit a consultant workflow — per-evaluation pricing, not a subscription. Let’s do the first one together at no cost and you can tell me what it’s worth to your practice.”

Redirect pricing questions to value: “What does a bad franchise recommendation cost a client?”
